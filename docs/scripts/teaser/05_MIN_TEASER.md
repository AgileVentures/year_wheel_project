# 5-Minute Teaser Demo Script

**Purpose**: Lightning-fast intro to hook interest  
**Duration**: 5 minutes max  
**Best for**: Trade shows, cold outreach, initial discovery calls, elevator pitch situations  
**Goal**: Create "aha moment" and schedule longer demo

---

## Pre-Demo Setup (1 minute before)

### Environment
- [ ] Demo account with ONE pre-built wheel (impressive, full year of activities)
- [ ] Browser clean, zoom 100%
- [ ] Sidebar CLOSED initially for maximum visual impact
- [ ] Know their industry/role (if possible)

### Mental Preparation
**Key insight**: You have 5 minutes to make them say "Show me more." Focus on ONE killer feature: **visual annual planning with drag-and-drop editing.**

---

## MINUTE 1: The Problem Hook

### Open with Pain Point

**Say:**
> "Quick question: How do you currently plan your year?"

*Listen to 10-second answer. Common responses:*
- "Excel spreadsheets"
- "Google Calendar"
- "Notion/Asana"
- "We don't really..."

### Acknowledge the Pain

**For spreadsheets:**
> "Right, so you've got rows and columns, but you can't actually SEE the year flow, can you?"

**For calendars:**
> "Okay, so day-by-day view. Ever try to see your entire year at once?"

**For project tools:**
> "Makes sense. But when your boss asks 'What's our Q2 look like?' can you answer in 10 seconds?"

**For "we don't":**
> "You're not alone. Most teams piece it together from memory and scattered notes."

---

## MINUTE 2: The "Aha" Moment

### Reveal the Wheel

**Screen share the pre-built wheel. Let them see it for 3 seconds in silence.**

**Then say:**
> "This is YearWheel. Your entire year. One screen."

**Slowly rotate the wheel 360 degrees:**
> "All 12 months, all your projects, campaigns, events - whatever you're planning - in a circular calendar."

### Quick Visual Tour (30 seconds)

**Point while describing:**
- **Months**: "Inner ring: January through December"
- **Rings/Bands**: "Each band is a category - marketing, sales, operations, whatever"
- **Colored segments**: "Each colored arc is a project or event"
- **Length of arc**: "Length shows duration - short events are small, year-long initiatives wrap around"

**Key line:**
> "You can see your entire year's strategy at a glance. No scrolling. No hunting through tabs."

---

## MINUTE 3: The Killer Feature

### Set Up the Scenario

**Say:**
> "But here's where it gets really good. Plans change constantly, right? Someone says 'Move that launch to Q3 instead of Q2.'"

**Point to an activity in Q2:**
> "Let's say this product launch needs to move..."

### Execute the Drag

**Click and drag the activity from Q2 to Q3 (slowly and deliberately):**

*Wait 2 seconds in silence after you drop it. Let the visual sink in.*

**Then say:**
> "That's it. Dates updated. No forms. No spreadsheet formulas. Just drag it."

### Show the Power

**Click sidebar to show activity details:**
> "Start date, end date - all recalculated automatically."

**Hover over edge of activity until cursor changes to ‚Üî:**
> "Need it to run longer? Drag the edge."

**Drag the edge to extend/shrink:**
> "Done. That fast."

**Key insight to share:**
> "What takes 5 minutes in Excel takes 5 SECONDS in YearWheel. And everyone on your team sees the change immediately."

## MINUTE 4: The Use Cases

### Make It Personal

**Ask:**
> "What would you use this for? [pause for answer]"

*If they engage, build on their answer. If not, offer examples:*

**For business leaders:**
> "Most teams use it for annual planning - all departments' initiatives in one place. Marketing sees what Sales is doing, Product knows when launches happen."

**For project managers:**
> "Perfect for multi-project visibility. See all your projects, spot resource conflicts, adjust timelines with a drag."

**For HR/Operations:**
> "Great for recurring events - quarterly reviews, monthly all-hands, training cycles. Plot it once, see the pattern."

**For consultants/agencies:**
> "Clients love it - show them their full campaign calendar in one visual. Way more impressive than a spreadsheet."

### Show Presentation Mode (30 seconds)

**Say:**
> "And when you present this to executives or clients..."

**Click to hide 1-2 rings using checkboxes:**
> "Hide details they don't need to see..."

**Click Export:**
> "Export as PNG, PDF, or SVG..."

**Say:**
> "Into your deck in 2 clicks. Professional visual that actually communicates the plan."

## MINUTE 5: Close for Next Step

### The Qualification Question

**Ask:**
> "Could you see your team using something like this?"

*Listen to their response:*

### Response A: "Yes, definitely!" (High Interest)

**Immediate action:**
> "Perfect. Let me send you a free trial link right now - you can build your first wheel in 15 minutes. And let's schedule 15 minutes next week so I can show you the collaboration features and answer any questions. Does [day/time] work?"

**If they say yes to meeting:**
- Get their email
- Confirm meeting time
- Send trial link and Quick Start guide immediately after call

### Response B: "Maybe, tell me more..." (Medium Interest)

**Ask:**
> "What would make this a 'definitely yes' for you?"

*Listen to their objections/questions:*
- **Price**: "Free tier gives you 2 wheels and 3 team members. Most people start there."
- **Features**: "What specific feature do you need? [Listen, then address]"
- **Timing**: "When would be a better time? Can I check back then?"
- **Need to see more**: "Makes sense. Can I show you a 15-minute full demo? [Suggest time]"

### Response C: "Not really..." (Low Interest)

**Quick qualify-out:**
> "No worries. Can I ask - what are you using now that's working well?"

*Listen. If they truly don't have a planning pain point:*

**Say:**
> "Makes sense. Keep us in mind if your planning process gets painful. Here's my email if you ever want to revisit. Best of luck!"

**Move on. Don't oversell.**

## Script Variations

### For Virtual Trade Shows / Webinars
- Pre-load wheel, minimize clicking
- Emphasize visual impact (since everyone sees screen clearly)
- Use chat to drop links
- Keep it to 3 minutes if multiple demos in queue

### For In-Person Trade Shows
- Use tablet/large screen for visual impact
- Let THEM drag something (physical touch is powerful)
- Have QR code ready for trial signup
- Collect business cards, not just emails

### For Cold Outreach Calls
- Ask permission first: "Do you have 5 minutes for me to show you something visual?"
- If no screenshare possible, describe it vividly
- Focus on pain point heavily
- Goal is to schedule a proper demo, not close on call

### For Warm Referrals
- Mention who referred you: "[Name] thought you'd find this useful for [use case]"
- Skip some of the qualifying questions (they're pre-qualified)
- Can go slightly longer (7-8 minutes) if they're engaged

## Pro Tips

**Silence is powerful**: After you drag an activity, PAUSE. Let them process the visual. Don't fill the silence immediately.

**One feature only**: Resist urge to show everything. The 5-minute demo is ONLY about "visual planning + drag-and-drop". Save collaboration, AI, integrations for the longer demo.

**Watch their eyes**: If they lean in, slow down and let them absorb. If they look confused, ask "Does this make sense so far?"

**End early if you've hooked them**: If they say "This is exactly what we need!" at minute 3, STOP the demo. Say "Want to see more right now or should I schedule a full walkthrough?" Don't risk over-demoing.

**Mobile tip**: If demoing on a phone/tablet, use landscape mode for wheel visibility. Zoom controls work better on touch screens.
